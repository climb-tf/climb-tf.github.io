##  Long Jumping
By strafing left or right while in mid-air, you gain speed. We can utilize this speed gain to increase the distance of our jumps by chaining strafes. The speed we gain in air and how fast we can strafe is determined by the air-acceleration. 

###  Important Detail: Dead Strafing
When jumping in TF2, there is a period where your acceleration in the air is reduced to 25%.

- The first 3 ticks after crouch-jumping you have full air-acceleration.
- From there on, until the apex of your jump, you have a 75% reduction of your air-acceleration. During this period, you should minimize your strafing.
- When you begin to fall, your air-acceleration will return to normal.

** Note: the ticks are different when your jump is affected by something else (such as launch pads)

** Note: The dead strafe period does not apply when walking off a surface without jumping.  

A few of the many examples of gaps that can be cleared with long jumping:  
- There is a 298 hu gap and a 300 hu gap on kz_h2kb_a2.
- There is a 300 hu gap on kz_tileworld.
- There is a 300 hu gap on kz_laza_rc1.
- There is a 302 hu gap on kz_atlantis.
- There is a 300 hu gap and a 302 hu gap on climb_luzon.
- There is a nearly 306 hu gap on cosmic_q4.
- There is a 308 hu gap on kz_frostline.
- There is a 308 hu gap on kz_swashbuckle.  

##  Pixel Walking
On ramps/slanted surfaces bordering a wall that are not moved into it can be walked ontop of. There a few ways to be able to get ontop of it.
- Look into the wall holding W and an A or D key. 
- Looking at 4 degree angle holding A or D.

Some examples of places where pixel walking is useful:  
- There are pixel walks towards the beginning of climb_luzon.
- There is a skip at the end of kz_outsideday that involves jumping from the top of the ramp.
- On kz_sector, there are a few pixel walks including a jump from a pixel walk expected to be done with hsw pre.  

##  Wallstrafing
Holding A or D (which ever key moves towards *into* the wall), jumping, continuing to hold the strafe key, and looking 4° away from the wall. This will allow you to gain speed in the air while along side a wall without strafing.  

Some examples of places where wallstrafing is useful for 107% speed classes:  
- The easiest way to do the pl_pier jump on climb_payload_a11_fixed is by wallstrafing.
- The easiest way to clear the 300 hu gap on kz_h2kb_a2 is with a wallstrafe.
- There is a jump on climb_luzon from pixel walk to pixel walk that requires wallstrafing.
- The easiest way to clear the 302 hu gap on climb_luzon is with a wallstrafe.
- A pixel walk on kz_sector needs to be landed on from a wallstrafe.
- A jump at the beginning of kz_slyvan requires a wallstrafe.  

##  Crouch Wallstrafing
While crouch walking against a wall, hold W and the strafe key that moves you towards the wall while looking roughly 8 degrees into the wall. This will allow you to move faster while crouch walking.  

Some examples of places where crouch wallstrafing is useful for 107% speed classes:  
- The tunnels that say "DETAIL OR DISTRACTION" on the inner walls on jump_mutegod can be crawled through faster with crouch wallstrafing.
- When you are forced into a crouch in the hollow prisms on jump_mutegod, you must crouch wallstrafe to get to the blue platform.
- The last jump on kz_wardslip requires decent pre from a crouch.
- The broken pipe you enter the plant from on kz_sector forces you to crouch. You need good pre to make it to the next platform.  

##  73 Jump
There are 3 ways to increase your height in TF2 past the normal 72.2 unit max jump height. 

### 72.9
Performing a single crouched bunnyhop will net you an additional +0.6 units of height.

### 73.6
Perform 2 bhops, the first one uncrouched, and the second one should be a crouched bhop. This will net you an additional 1.4 units of height.

###  74 Jump
This is only possible when on classes that are able to change their max speed. This can be achieved with items that augment your speed such as, the disguise kit, dead ringer, Gloves of Running Urgently, etc...

For simplicity, when referring to bhops they will be uncrouched unless explicitly stated.

Some methods on Spy:
#### 300 -> Heavy
1. Disguise as a 300u/s speed class
2. Run and **strafe** into the jump with a speed ranging from 301 u/s to 316 u/s
3. change disguise to heavy
4. bhop
5. crouch bhop

#### 320 -> Soldier
1. Disguise as scout (or not at all, swapping disguises is faster than disguising from none)
2. run and jump without strafing
3. change disguise to soldier in mid-air
4. bhop
5. crouched bhop

| Class      | Speed Required |
| Heavy      | 300.35-316.66 u/s |
| Soldier    | 313-330 u/s |
| Demo       | 365.67-385.52 u/s |
| Py/Eng/Sni | 391-413 u/s |
| Spy        | 417.92 - 440.6 u/s |


| Dead Ringer | Speed Required |
| Heavy       | 420.35-445.66 u/s |


Some examples of when getting more height is useful:  
- Crouch-jumping instead of scrolling is better when statting bhops.
- Statting MBH is best done with the 73.6 hu high jump. Also, it is difficult, but not impossible, to do the 74 hu high MBH without disguising.
- There is a 72.5 hu tall platform and a 73 hu tall platform on jump_mutegod.
- There is a 73 hu tall box on kz_sector.
- There is a 72.5 hu tall platform at the end of kz_swashbuckle.
- There is a 73 hu tall platform on kz_mantle.
- The easiest way to do the beginning of kz_baxter_tf2 as Heavy is with the 73.6 hu high jump.  

##  Jumpbug
A jump bug is unducking at the same time you touch the ground and jumping. This lets you avoid fall damage and bypass certain triggers.  

Some examples of when jumpbugs are useful:  
- The trigger over the slime on kz_crash_rc1 can be avoided with a jumpbug.
- There is a huge skip on kz_abydos that requires an uncrouched perf on the hot floors followed by a jumpbug.
- The easiest way to do the jump before the long speed buffed fall as Heavy on climb_eientei is to jumpbug.  

##  Surfs
Height can be maintained and speed can be gained by strafing into sloped surfaces that cannot be stood on ("ramps"). Look slightly away from the ramp to build speed. Landing on a ramp ("boarding") abruptly loses speed. To maintain as much speed as possible, board smoothly. Unlike how it works in dedicated surf servers, massive height cannot be gained by turning into the ramp with high speed in vanilla TF2.  

Some examples of surfs:  
- Towards the end of kz_cowland, there is a huge surf ramp.
- There are some surf ramps towards the end of kz_descent.
- There are surf ramps at the end of kz_frostline.
- A "head surf" with inverted gravity is on climb_eientei. There are a few other surf ramps as well.
- The building labeled "D" at the end of pl_snowycoast can be surfed across. This jump is also on climb_payload_a11_fixed.
- There are a couple of surf ramps on kz_moondust. On one of them, you need to turn left abruptly in the middle of the ramp.
- There are a few surf ramps on kz_jollyclimb.
- An easy surf ramp takes you from point A to point B on kz_oradon.
- One spot on kz_mantle is done by surfing very slowly. Another spot on the map requires you to get lots of speed. After you jump into the earth you surf on the surrounding rock.
- There are many difficult surfs on kz_sector.  

##  Surf ups
Very steep slopes can be scaled even if they are too tall to climb with a regular crouch-jump. Simply hold W and crouch-jump directly into the ones that aren't steep or tall. For harder slides:
1. Face the slide at an angle. The optimal angle depends on the slide.
2. Run towards the slide by holding W.
3. Just before reaching the slide, strafe towards it and release W without moving your mouse.
4. Hold the strafe key until you are at the top.
5. For even more height, you can slightly flick your mouse in the opposite direction of your strafe key as you move up the ramp.

Some examples of where you can surf up ramps:  
- There are several maps showcased in climb_payload_a11_fixed that have useful instances of slides. Some jumps that can be applied in actual games are jumping out of stream on pl_swiftwater and accessing a flank on pl_snowycoast.
- There is a slide on climb_luzon after the hsw pre.
- The very first jump on kz_slyvan is a slide. There are several smaller slides throughout the map.
- There is a 103 hu tall slide on kz_sector.  

##  Ledgegrab / Glidestep
You ordinarily jump onto a higher platform by jumping above it and falling onto it. You can skip falling onto it by uncrouching, immediately putting you in a grounded state on the platform. Some platforms are the wrong height for this to work.  

Some examples of places where glidesteps are useful:  
- The very end of climb work can be done faster with glidesteps.
- The bonus on kz_bedoma is intended to be done as a series of glidesteps.
- Lots of platforms can be climbed faster on kz_cavern with glidesteps.  

##  Ctaps / MJ
Some jumps have low ceilings that are made possible or easier by jumping lower (ctapping). To ctap, jump while uncrouching. This isn't possible from a full crouch, but is possible if you briefly tap crouch without holding it down long enough for your walking speed to be reduced.
